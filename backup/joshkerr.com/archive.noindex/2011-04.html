Cortex is an amazing Tumblr extension for Chrome

￼

I am a big fan of Chrome and I like to blog on Tumblr. Then along came Cortex (http://www.cortexapp.com) which changed the way I blog. Previously I used to write a blog article once or twice a week. These infrequent articles were lengthy and took me a while to write. Back in those days I was using Wordpress which seemed more like a writer’s blog platform. Then I switched to Tumblr which allowed me to easily re-blog other Tumblr articles. Now I could easily blog a few articles a day, but my selection was rather limited for doing re-blogs because it only worked on other Tumblogs.

Now enter Cortex. It is a simple Chrome Extension which lets you quickly blog content that you see on the page. Simply position the cursor over the item you want to share. Press and hold the mouse button for a few seconds and a wheel appears letting you blog it to Tumblr, Facebook, Twitter or Instapaper. It does a great job formatting the images and even allows you to add text if you want it.

I’ve used it without a problem to blog video too. Just browse to a youtube video and use Cortex to create a Tumblr video post.

This app allows me to blog 5 − 10 times a day. My Tumblog looks more like a Twitter stream which I much prefer to the lengthy articles of yesterday.

Mars Edit for power users - (Markdown)

You are a Mars Edit user and you want to start writing your posts in Markdown but you aren’t sure where to start. You want the preview feature to show the actual theme on your blow. You need quick access to macros but aren’t sure how to set them.

If you fit into any of these categories, this post will help you. I’ve managed to configure a good workflow around MarsEdit that makes the tedium of writing blog entries rather easy. Or easier, you still have to write the content.

Using Markdown

The first thing you need to do to write in Markdown is to tell MarsEdit to default to the HTML edit view.

Just check the option for “HTML Text.” This will make MarsEdit default to the HTML mode which means your Markdown syntax doesn’t get ruined in the visual editor.

Next go into the settings for your blog and change the Default Preview Filter to Markdown.

Customize the Macros

I created custom macros to create links, references, blockquote, etc.. You can get to the customize menu from the “Format” toolbar. Just choose “edit” and create a bunch of macros. This really speeds up adding images, links, etc..

The image above shows some of the custom macros and the editor used to create them.

Conclusion

If you follow these tips, you’ll have a faster workflow for creating new content for your blog. Feel free to suggest new tips in the comments below.

Being productive on an airplane - GTD

How do you get things done at 35,000 feet on an airplane with no power and no Internet access? Its actually not that hard as long as you spend a little bit of time preparing before the flight. My pre-flight preparation takes about 10 minutes and allows me to get a lot of work done.

### Power

You don’t have a power adapter on your flight so you are going to need to get the most out of your notebook’s battery. I recommend charging your computer before the flight. It helps if you bring along an extra battery like the one from [Hyper Mac][], but you can get surprising battery run times by lowering your display brightness, disabling any wireless signals like Bluetooth & Wifi and keeping CPU intensive & disk intensive tasks at bay.

I also recommend taking a look at [Watts][], it is a Mac program that helps you keep your battery properly exercised so that it gives you the longest and best performance possible.

### No Internet

Some flights have Wifi Internet access, but you don’t need it to get a lot done. If you have an offline reader or use an app like [Instapaper][] or [ReadItLater][], you can pre-download content to read while you are offline. I integrate Instapaper with Google Reader and spend some time tagging articles that I’m interested in reading while in flight.

I also like [Reeder][] which can sync my Google Reader account to my notebook so that I can read the articles while in flight.

### Comfort

Having enough space to be comfortable is tough to achieve and not necessarily possible on all flights, but there are some things you can do:

* Search for empty flights. You typically can get an empty seat next to you
* Opt for the extra leg room seats
* Look for emergency exit seats

### Conclusion

With a little bit of preparation you can get a lot done on your flight.

How to get Spotify in the US on your iPhone

If you are like me, you’ve been drooling over the Spotify service which is only available in Europe.  It lets you listen to a massive selection of music without having to buy it.  It has social sharing built in, and it works great on your iPhone.  If only it was available in the US.

If you try to register or download the software from a computer within the US you are greeted with this lovely message:

What if you live in Europe and you happen to be visiting the US?  What if you need access to the software from outside the UK?  Well, it turns out that you can.  Even from within the US.  So how does a US person access the service?  Well, by pretending you live in Europe.

I was able to activate the service both on my computer and my iPhone.  I listen to it all the time and it works perfectly.

I believe that the only reason Spotify doesn’t work in the US is that they haven’t secured the licensing required to operate here.  I imagine that sometime this year they will secure the rights and launch the service.  Rumors suggest this will happen sooner than later.

How to get Spotify in the US

First you need to create a Spotify account.  To do this you will need to fool Spotify in thinking that you are coming from a UK server.  I used a Proxy server to do this.  There are a ton on the Internet, just do a Google search for “UK Proxy server” and you’ll find one.

I used this one: http://www.daveproxy.co.uk/

Browse to the Spotify site and create a new account.  Make sure to choose the free service (they won’t let you buy a subscription.  More on that later.)  Once you have your account, it is time to download the software.

Using the same proxy, download and install the desktop software.  For the iPhone software we are going to need a new iTunes account.  No problem.

Launch iTunes and select the iTunes Store.  At the bottom you should see a link to change country.

Click it and register a new account.  I used a UK address I found in google.  It doesn’t matter which one you use, you aren’t going to add a credit card or use this account for anything other than downloading the client.  Make sure to use a real email account that isn’t already associated with another iTunes account.  You will need to activate the account via email.

Do not add a credit card to your iTunes Account.  When you are done created it, activate your account via email.  Now you should be able to download the Spotify client.

Sync it to your iPhone.  Now comes the last step.  You need to upgrade to a Premium subscription in order to use the iPhone client.  You are going to need to hop on to Ebay and purchase a subscription.

I purchased mine from eleanor_1257.  This person responded really quickly and within a few minutes I had my premium account.  You’ll need to give away your login so that they can login and activate your account via their UK credit card.  Since I didn’t have any personal information in my spotify account, I had no problem doing that.

I highly recommend the Spotify service.  It is a great way to enjoy a massive amount of music without needing to shell out an arm and a leg.  Consider me satisfied.
