Being productive on an airplane - GTD

How do you get things done at 35,000 feet on an airplane with no power and no Internet access? Its actually not that hard as long as you spend a little bit of time preparing before the flight. My pre-flight preparation takes about 10 minutes and allows me to get a lot of work done.

### Power

You don’t have a power adapter on your flight so you are going to need to get the most out of your notebook’s battery. I recommend charging your computer before the flight. It helps if you bring along an extra battery like the one from [Hyper Mac][], but you can get surprising battery run times by lowering your display brightness, disabling any wireless signals like Bluetooth & Wifi and keeping CPU intensive & disk intensive tasks at bay.

I also recommend taking a look at [Watts][], it is a Mac program that helps you keep your battery properly exercised so that it gives you the longest and best performance possible.

### No Internet

Some flights have Wifi Internet access, but you don’t need it to get a lot done. If you have an offline reader or use an app like [Instapaper][] or [ReadItLater][], you can pre-download content to read while you are offline. I integrate Instapaper with Google Reader and spend some time tagging articles that I’m interested in reading while in flight.

I also like [Reeder][] which can sync my Google Reader account to my notebook so that I can read the articles while in flight.

### Comfort

Having enough space to be comfortable is tough to achieve and not necessarily possible on all flights, but there are some things you can do:

* Search for empty flights. You typically can get an empty seat next to you
* Opt for the extra leg room seats
* Look for emergency exit seats

### Conclusion

With a little bit of preparation you can get a lot done on your flight.
